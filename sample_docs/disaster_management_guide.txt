DISASTER MANAGEMENT & EMERGENCY RESPONSE GUIDE
===============================================
A Comprehensive Reference for Emergency Preparedness and Response

CHAPTER 1: EARTHQUAKE RESPONSE PROCEDURES
==========================================

1.1 Immediate Actions During an Earthquake
-------------------------------------------
When an earthquake strikes, follow the DROP, COVER, and HOLD ON protocol:

DROP: Get down on your hands and knees immediately. This position protects you from being knocked down and allows you to move if necessary.

COVER: Take cover under a sturdy desk or table if available. If no shelter is nearby, cover your face and head with your arms and crouch in an inside corner of the building.

HOLD ON: Hold on to your shelter (or to your head and neck) until the shaking stops. Be prepared to move with your shelter if the shaking shifts it around.

1.2 After the Earthquake
--------------------------
- Check for injuries and provide first aid as needed
- Check for hazards: fires, gas leaks, damage to electrical systems
- If indoors, watch for falling plaster, bricks, lighting fixtures
- Open doors carefully as they may be warped and spring open
- Stay away from beaches and low-lying coastal areas (tsunami risk)
- Expect aftershocks — secondary shaking events that can be damaging
- Do not use elevators after an earthquake
- Stay out of damaged buildings

1.3 Earthquake Severity Scale (Richter)
-----------------------------------------
- Magnitude 2.5 or less: Usually not felt
- Magnitude 2.5 to 5.4: Often felt, minor damage
- Magnitude 5.5 to 6.0: Slight damage to buildings
- Magnitude 6.1 to 6.9: May cause heavy damage in populated areas
- Magnitude 7.0 to 7.9: Major earthquake, serious damage
- Magnitude 8.0 or greater: Great earthquake, total destruction near epicenter

CHAPTER 2: FLOOD RESPONSE AND EVACUATION
==========================================

2.1 Flash Flood Safety
-----------------------
Flash floods are among the most dangerous natural disasters. Follow these guidelines:

NEVER attempt to walk, swim, or drive through flood waters. Turn Around, Don't Drown.
- Six inches of moving water can knock you down
- One foot of moving water can sweep your vehicle away
- Two feet of rushing water can carry most vehicles away

2.2 Flood Warning Categories
------------------------------
- Flash Flood Watch: Conditions are favorable for flash flooding. Be alert and prepared to take action.
- Flash Flood Warning: Flash flooding is occurring or imminent. Take immediate action to protect life and property.
- Flash Flood Emergency: A flash flood is occurring. This is an extremely dangerous and life-threatening situation.

2.3 Evacuation Procedures
---------------------------
When instructed to evacuate:
1. Leave immediately — do not wait to see if flooding begins
2. Disconnect electrical appliances if safe to do so
3. Do not touch electrical equipment if wet or standing in water
4. Move to higher ground immediately
5. Take emergency kit including water, food, medications
6. Follow designated evacuation routes
7. Do not return home until authorities declare it safe

2.4 Flood Emergency Kit
-------------------------
Essential items to prepare before flood season:
- Water (one gallon per person per day, three-day supply)
- Non-perishable food items
- Battery-powered radio and flashlights
- First aid kit and medications
- Waterproof containers for important documents
- Rope, whistle, and signal mirror
- Life jackets for each family member

CHAPTER 3: WILDFIRE RESPONSE
==============================

3.1 Evacuation Warning Levels
-------------------------------
Level 1 - READY: Be aware of danger in your area. Prepare to leave.
Level 2 - SET: Be prepared to leave at a moment's notice. Have your belongings ready to go.
Level 3 - GO: Leave immediately. Danger in your area is current or imminent.

3.2 Wildfire Evacuation Checklist
------------------------------------
Before leaving:
- Close all windows, vents, and doors to prevent draft
- Remove flammable window shades, curtains, and lightweight drapes
- Move flammable furniture to the center of the room
- Turn off gas at the meter
- Connect garden hoses to outdoor spigots
- Place lawn sprinklers on roof and water the roof
- Do not block the driveway (emergency vehicles need access)

During evacuation:
- Drive with headlights on
- Keep car windows and air vents closed
- Drive away from fire — not around it
- Have destination in mind and alternative routes planned

3.3 Protecting Your Home from Wildfire
----------------------------------------
Create defensible space:
- Zone 1 (0-30 feet): Keep clear of combustible materials
- Zone 2 (30-100 feet): Reduce density of trees and plants
- Use fire-resistant building materials
- Clear roof and gutters of debris
- Install ember-resistant vents

CHAPTER 4: HURRICANE AND TROPICAL STORM PREPAREDNESS
======================================================

4.1 Hurricane Categories (Saffir-Simpson Scale)
-------------------------------------------------
Category 1: Winds 74-95 mph — Some damage
Category 2: Winds 96-110 mph — Extensive damage
Category 3: Winds 111-129 mph — Devastating damage (Major hurricane)
Category 4: Winds 130-156 mph — Catastrophic damage (Major hurricane)
Category 5: Winds 157+ mph — Catastrophic damage (Major hurricane)

4.2 Hurricane Preparedness Timeline
--------------------------------------
72+ hours before landfall:
- Review and update family emergency plan
- Stock emergency supplies for 7 days
- Fill gas tank and cash withdrawals
- Make sure phone/electronics are charged

48 hours before landfall:
- Put up storm shutters or board windows
- Move outdoor furniture inside
- Review evacuation routes
- Fill bathtubs with water for toilet flushing

24 hours before landfall:
- Evacuate if in Zones A, B, or C, or if ordered
- Secure all remaining outdoor items
- Make final contact with family members

4.3 Shelter-in-Place During Hurricane
---------------------------------------
If not evacuating:
- Stay indoors on the lowest floor away from windows
- If in a two-story house, go to the lowest floor
- Close all interior doors
- Take refuge in small interior rooms, closets, or hallways
- Lie on the floor under a table or another sturdy object
- Do not go outside during the eye of the storm

CHAPTER 5: MEDICAL EMERGENCY RESPONSE
=======================================

5.1 Basic First Aid Protocols
-------------------------------

Severe Bleeding:
1. Apply firm, direct pressure to the wound
2. Use a clean cloth or bandage if available
3. Apply pressure continuously — do not lift to check wound
4. If limb, apply tourniquet 2-3 inches above wound if bleeding is severe
5. Seek emergency medical help immediately

CPR for Adults:
1. Call 911 immediately
2. Begin chest compressions: push hard and fast in center of chest
3. Rate: 100-120 compressions per minute
4. Depth: at least 2 inches
5. Allow full chest recoil between compressions
6. If trained: provide 2 rescue breaths after every 30 compressions

Treating Shock:
1. Lay the person down and elevate their legs (unless head, neck, or back injury)
2. Keep the person warm using a blanket
3. Do not give them anything to eat or drink
4. Get medical help immediately

5.2 Disaster-Specific Medical Concerns
----------------------------------------

Crush Injury Syndrome:
- Occurs when body parts are compressed for extended periods
- Requires immediate medical attention upon extrication
- Do not move victim if spinal injury suspected

Hypothermia:
- Move to warm, dry location immediately
- Remove wet clothing
- Cover with warm blankets including head
- Provide warm beverages if conscious (no alcohol)
- Seek medical help for severe hypothermia

Heat Exhaustion/Stroke:
- Move to cool, shaded area
- Remove excess clothing
- Apply cool, wet cloths to skin
- Fan person or move to air-conditioned area
- Give cool water to drink (if conscious)
- Call 911 for heat stroke (confusion, loss of consciousness)

CHAPTER 6: CHEMICAL AND HAZMAT INCIDENTS
==========================================

6.1 Immediate Response to Chemical Exposure
---------------------------------------------
- Evacuate the area immediately upwind
- Call 911 and report the incident
- Do not re-enter contaminated area
- If exposed to chemicals: remove clothing, flush with water 15 minutes
- Shelter in place if evacuation is not possible:
  - Close and lock all windows and doors
  - Turn off heating/air conditioning systems
  - Seal gaps with wet towels or plastic sheeting

6.2 Shelter-in-Place Protocol for Hazmat
------------------------------------------
1. Go inside immediately
2. Close all windows and doors
3. Turn off all fans, air conditioning, forced-air heating systems
4. Close fireplace dampers
5. Use plastic sheeting and duct tape to seal all openings
6. Turn on local radio/TV for emergency updates
7. If told to evacuate, do so immediately

CHAPTER 7: TSUNAMI PREPAREDNESS
=================================

7.1 Natural Warning Signs
---------------------------
- Strong ground shaking
- Loud ocean roar
- Ocean water receding unusually far
- Sudden rise in water level

7.2 Tsunami Response
---------------------
IF YOU ARE IN A TSUNAMI HAZARD ZONE:
- Move immediately to higher ground or inland
- Do not wait for official warning
- A tsunami can arrive minutes after an earthquake
- A series of waves may continue for hours
- Stay away from beaches until official all-clear is given

7.3 Tsunami Wave Characteristics
----------------------------------
- Travel at 500-600 mph in deep ocean
- Slow to 20-30 mph near shore
- Can be 100+ feet high
- Multiple waves may strike over several hours
- Inland surge can travel miles
